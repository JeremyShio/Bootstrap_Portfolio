October, 2009

My first Homebrew on iOS

I remember watching on TV In August 2007, seventeen-year-old George Hotz became the first person reported to remove the SIM lock on an iPhone. What caught my attention was the fact that He traded his second unlocked 8 GB iPhone to Terry Daidone, the founder of CertiCell, for a Nissan 350Z and three 8GB iPhones. What an astounding trade if I do say so myself! Little did I know that In October 2009, Hotz released blackra1n. It was compatible with all iPhone and iPod Touch devices running iOS 3.1.2. Soon enough it was the program I would use to get Cydia on my iPod Touch! With Cydia I entered the world of Homebrew and was able to manage custom repositories on my device. I suppose this is where my lust for learning more began.
